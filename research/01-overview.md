## What is PSI Theory?

PSI (Œ®) Theory, developed by German psychologist **Dietrich D√∂rner** at the University of Bamberg in the 1990s, is a cognitive architecture that models human action regulation by integrating:

- **Motivation** (drives/needs that create pressure)
- **Emotion** (affect/emergent from motivation-cognition interactions)
- **Cognition** (planning, perception, memory)

### Core Insight

The theory posits that intelligent behavior emerges from the interaction between:
1. **Demands/Urges** - Basic needs that create motivational pressure
2. **Motives** - Active goals competing for control
3. **Cognitive Modulators** - Parameters that adjust processing style
4. **Affect & Emotions** - Emergent states from the interaction of the above

### Key Distinction from Traditional AI

Unlike goal-directed AI agents that simply optimize objectives, PSI agents have **needs that drain over time** (like hunger, affiliation, curiosity) that create internal pressure. This is much closer to biological intelligence and means the agent's behavior is driven by internal states, not just external goals.
